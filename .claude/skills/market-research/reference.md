```markdown
- **Target customer**: [who they serve]

```

## Market Sizing Methodologies

### Top-Down
1. Start with total industry size (from reports)
2. Narrow by geography, segment, use case
3. Apply realistic penetration rate
4. **Risk**: Tends to overestimate

### Bottom-Up
1. Estimate price per customer
2. Estimate number of reachable customers
3. Multiply and discount by conversion rate
4. **Better for**: SOM estimation

### Comparable
1. Find similar company in adjacent market
2. Look at their revenue and market share
3. Adjust for market size differences
4. **Good for**: Sanity checking other methods

## Confidence Levels

| Level | Definition | Evidence Required |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| High | Multiple corroborating sources, quantitative data | 3+ independent sources |
| Medium | Some data, reasonable extrapolation | 1-2 sources + logical reasoning |
| Low | Limited data, significant assumptions | Expert opinion or analogies only |

## Source Quality Hierarchy
1. Industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey, Statista)
2. Company financials and SEC filings
3. Peer-reviewed research
4. Tech press (TechCrunch, The Information)
5. Blog posts and social media (lowest reliability)

## Common Pitfalls
- Confusing TAM with SOM (SOM is typically 1-5% of TAM for startups)
- Ignoring indirect competitors
- Assuming market share is static
- Cherry-picking favorable data
- Ignoring switching costs
